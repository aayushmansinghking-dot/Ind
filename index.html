Biography

Rani Lakshmi Bai was born on 19 November 1828 in Varanasi as Manikarnika Tambe. She was trained in horse riding, sword fighting and showed bravery from a young age. After marrying Maharaja Gangadhar Rao of Jhansi, she became the Queen of Jhansi. When the British refused to accept her adopted son as heir, she fought to protect her kingdom with fearless determination.

Role in India’s Freedom Struggle

Rani Lakshmi Bai played a pivotal role in India’s First War of Independence (1857). She led her army with courage and fought fiercely against British forces, motivating people with her patriotism and strength. Riding into battle with her infant tied to her back became one of the most iconic images of resistance in Indian history.
